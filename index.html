KAYAK: Trailblazing in the Travel Industry

Since 2004, KAYAK has been a trailblazer in the travel industry, introducing meta search for travel and processing billions of queries annually. The platform aggregates information from hundreds of travel sites, providing users with a comprehensive view of flights, hotels, rental cars, and vacation packages. From a small team of 14, KAYAK has grown into a company with over 1,000 travel enthusiasts operating across 7 international brands. Acquired by Booking Holdings in 2013, KAYAK continues to lead in online travel, making it easier for millions of travelers worldwide to confidently explore the world.

Kayak Features

At KAYAK, the mission is to simplify hotel searches, providing users with the information they need to make confident booking decisions. Here’s an overview of how KAYAK’s hotel services function: • Search and Compare: Conducting a simple search on KAYAK allows users to scan prices across numerous hotel booking sites. Users can compare options for the same hotel and choose where to book, whether directly with the hotel or through another platform. • Fee-Free Booking: Hotel prices come directly from the booking sites to the user’s screen, with no extra fees from KAYAK. The service is entirely free for users, as hotels pay KAYAK when users are redirected to their sites. • Advertisement Display: Travel-related advertisements may be displayed on KAYAK’s site, with some companies compensating KAYAK for user clicks.

Kayak Recommendations

1. Recommended Sorting: Default sorting is based on KAYAK’s ’Recommended’ algorithm, considering parameters like price, guest ratings, and popularity. KAYAK also factors in the average revenue potential from each hotel result. 2. Sorting Options: Users can tailor their search with filters and sorting options such as price, hotel class, review score, and distance. 3. Provider Visibility: The same deal may be available on several provider sites, all visible for user selection. KAYAK may highlight one main provider based on factors like customer popularity, ratings, or payments. 4. Price Sorting: A ’Price’ sort provides quick access to the cheapest and/or most expensive hotel deals.
